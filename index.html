Publications

Published (6th International Conference on Civil, Architecture, Urbanity Engineering, 2022):
Ali Haddadi, Mohammad Reza Nikoo. “A Histogram-based Image Segmentation using Fuzzy C-means and K-means and Trend Analysis using Mann-Kendall”.

Abstract:
A wide range of scientific fields rely on image segmentation methods for their success and progress. This study surveyed the performance of the k-means and fuzzy c-means approaches for image segmentation in detecting agricultural land use using the image of aerial maps. Also, the Mann-Kendall test was utilized for trend analysis of the results obtained from both techniques. These methods were applied to the map of Al Khaburah city, Oman. The results demonstrated that the percentage of the agricultural area declined from 2007 to 2021.


Published (12th International Congress on Civil Engineering, 2021):
Ali Haddadi , Mohammad Ali Yousefzadeh and Meisam Takalloozadeh. “Optimization of Truss Structures by Genetic Algorithm and Simulated Annealing Algorithm With Application of New Adaptive Interior Penalty Method”.

Abstract:
Optimization problems of structures are generally included in the category of non-linear optimization problems. One of the common methods in solving constrained optimization problems is to use penalty methods and convert them into unconstrained optimization problems. In conventional penalty methods, such as internal and external penalty methods, the value of the penalty coefficient is determined in each step to guarantee the convergence of the optimization problem. This is done in each step by decreasing or increasing the value of the penalty coefficient compared to the previous step. In a different and relatively new approach, the optimization algorithm determines this coefficient at each stage along with the design variables. Therefore, the penalty coefficient is entered and updated as a design variable in the optimization algorithm. In this research, truss problems were used as a case study to survey this approach using genetic and simulated annealing algorithms. Also, utilizing truss structures as optimization problems, these two optimization algorithms with a modified penalty function were compared in terms of speed and accuracy.
